GetScrumban Game Intro

Scrum is an incredibly simple, effective and highly-popular software development framework. Despite its simplicity, Scrum can be difficult to master.

Scrumban is also a simple framework, but is widely misunderstood. Some believe it to be a new software development framework that combines “the best” elements of Scrum and Kanban. Others believe it to be using kanban systems inside of Scrum. Others have yet different understandings.

Scrumban essentially provides a set of complementary tools and capabilities for teams using Scrum. It can be used to accelerate new adoptions, to help overcome common pitfalls & challenges, or to evolve a new framework that sustains value-delivery and agility over time.

The objective of this website is to help novices and experts alike improve their ability to amplify capabilities, overcome common challenges, and forge alternative paths to agility.

Why Scrumban?

Scrumban minimizes psychological barriers to change, and can fulfill the essential purposes of many Scrum roles and ceremonies as people and organizations evolve.

Scrumban adds new and different capabilities to Scrum environments, helping teams overcome common challenges when new perspectives or approaches are needed.

Scrumban helps to expand Agility beyond the team, improving shared understandings and shifting conversations away from process and toward service delivery.

Key Principles & Practices

Systems Thinking

Understanding and then minimizing the complexity of the systems in which we work is critical, and Scrumban's framework is founded upon systems thinking principles that bring these capabilities to the forefront. These understandings and capabilities are what allow us to increase leverage (doing more with less), produce more breakthroughs (innovation), and more easily solve problems.

Visualization

Human beings process complex information and relationships more effectively when they're visualized in some fashion. The Scrumban framework calls for us to visualize our workflow (the paths our work follows), our work policies ("rules" around how we work), the results of our efforts (metrics), and much more. These visualizations enhance shared understandings among teams and across organizations.

Risk Management

In seeking to strike a balance between our capacity to produce work and the demands placed upon us, we tend to overemphasize the importance of improving our capabilities. This is because we don't recognize we have the ability to influence the demands placed upon us through adaptive risk management techniques. The Scrumban framework helps us influence "upstream" activities to shape work demands as an additional means of achieving balance.

Pull vs. Push

The difference between a pull and a push system lies in how units of work are assigned to the person who will be carrying it out. In a push system, a manager or team leader allocates work among the people who will be doing it. In a pull system, the work that needs to be done is stored in a queue and idle team members are responsible for "pulling" the highest priority work and begin working on it, providing for better management of capabilities.
